Why this list

  • 01 Auto transport for military families is a different operational problem than a typical retail move. Orders define the timing window, not customer preference. PCS season concentrates demand on a small number of lanes (Norfolk, San Diego, Jacksonville, Fort Cavazos, Camp Pendleton) at predictable times of year. Carriers know this and pricing usually reflects it; and brokers without dedicated military programs can struggle to handle the constraints.
  • 02 Three things distinguish a broker that handles military moves well: a published military discount that applies without back-and-forth; flexibility around report-date pickup windows when orders shift; and a customer service team that has worked enough PCS season to know what the customer is asking for when they mention DITY or PPM.
  • 03 The seven companies on this page meet at least the first criterion (published military discount) and have visible operational history with military customers. Ranked order reflects depth of military programs alongside the general operational signals we apply to every company we review.

Methodology

What we measure for military auto transport.

01

Published military discount

Whether the company offers a published, applied-without-negotiation discount for active-duty military, veterans, or military spouses.

02

PCS timing flexibility

Whether the broker accommodates orders-driven pickup windows. PCS dates shift; brokers that lock customers into rigid pickup commitments tend to handle this less gracefully than ones built around military timelines.

03

Military lane coverage

Carrier network depth on the high-volume military lanes (Norfolk, San Diego, Jacksonville, Fort Cavazos, Camp Pendleton, Joint Base Lewis-McChord, and similar bases). Brokers with thin coverage on these lanes price higher and dispatch slower in PCS season.

04

Customer service literacy

Whether the customer service team understands DITY/PPM, BAH considerations, orders documentation, and the operational language military customers use. Verified through review platform narratives rather than self-reported case studies.
